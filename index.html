My research investigates the connections between managerial ability, labor scheduling decisions, automation, and firm performance. For more details on my papers, please visit the Research Section of my website.

As part of my research, I also work as a consultant for a tech company that develops and offers commercial AI tools to firms across various industries. I work for, and collaborate with this company, to empirically study the use of AI tools that automate a variety of business operations (e.g., demand forecasting, labor scheduling, inventory management). Some questions include:

  1. What are the effects of such AI tools on store performance?
  2. How do managers interact with AI tools? What are some of the challenges in this interaction?
  3. What guidance and training can we provide managers to better utilize such tools?
